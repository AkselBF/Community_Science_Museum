Exhibition spaces

Over the course of human history, science has developed from our early understanding of fire, wind, water, and earth to exploring everything from galaxies far away to the very building blocks of life itself.

In here, you can satiate your curiosity with the many facets science has to offer. Whether it's the cosmos, hearth or any other subject you're looking, It's in here.

Cosmology

Follow the journey of our solar exploration: from early Arab traders, to Galileo's telescope, to the latest exploration of the planets in our solar system.

Our team are on hand to give your students guided tours of the museum, teach them in our learning laboratory, and provide great video presentations that will excite and inspire them.

Evolution

For centuries, philosophers and scientists have wrestled with the question of our origins. Where do we come from and how did we get here?

Discover the origins of life on this planet and how the species we know today have evolved our time. You can also take some time to meet a few of the creatures who didn't survive, including dinosaurs.

See more

Biology & medicine

From micro-organisms to the human body, major breakthroughs in biology are offering us unique insights into the great wonders of the tiny world.

Over a series of exhibits we explore the history of medicine and take a look at some major breakthroughs including the discovery of penicillin and the first heart transplant.

See more

Robotics & AI

The information revolution is here and robotics and artificial intelligence are the science of the future.

From useful home applications of AI to industrial uses of robotics, the future is here. The newest technologies, the ways to make them a reality and future ideas all found here. As an example, you can even say hello and shake the hand of Rob the Robot.

See more

Ecology

As we learn more and more of the impact humans are having on the planet, the more important it becomes that scientists explore how to create a sustainable planetary future.

In our ecology section of the museum, we unpack the dangers we currently face including global warming, extinction of species, and pollution. But we also focus on the positive ways we can all contribute to a brighter future.
